What cookies are

Cookies are small text files that websites send to the user’s browser (or similar storage environments), where they are stored and sent back to the site on subsequent visits or to a site that recognises that cookie. Cookies are used for different purposes: some are necessary to make browsing efficient or to perform functions strictly related to delivering the service requested by the user; others may store preferences, enable aggregate statistical measurement or — in configurations different from the present Sites — profile users for advertising purposes.

Similar technologies (e.g. local storage, session storage, device identifiers, tracking pixels) may perform comparable functions: where they involve processing of personal data, they fall under privacy law and, where relevant, rules on cookies and electronic communications.

Types of cookies and tools used

1. Technical and necessary cookies

These cookies (or functionally equivalent tools) do not require consent because they are strictly necessary to enable navigation or to provide a functionality expressly requested by the user. Examples include:

  • Session cookies: typically deleted when the browser closes or after a server-defined inactivity period, used to maintain session state during a visit.
  • Functionality cookies: store certain preferences (e.g. language or interface settings) for subsequent visits where such features are active.
  • Persistent technical cookies: remain until expiry or manual deletion when technically necessary (e.g. non-invasive preference storage or load and security management).

These are generally first-party cookies set by the controller’s domain or managed on the controller’s behalf by hosting or infrastructure providers as mere technical processors, within the limits of contract and law.

2. Statistics and analytics (first-party proprietary system)

For traffic monitoring, the Site does not use third-party statistical cookies such as Google Analytics or similar tools that use profiling or cross-site tracking cookies on the Site’s domain.

Collection of information on session duration, visits, pages viewed and interaction events (e.g. clicks on contact buttons) is performed through a first-party proprietary system, with measures described in summary in the Privacy Policy, including:

  • generation of volatile technical identifiers (e.g. cryptographic fingerprints with time rotation) preventing reconstruction of behaviour beyond what statistics require;
  • minimisation, masking or truncation of IP addresses and user agents where applicable;
  • limited retention of temporary identifiers (e.g. up to 48 hours unless documented security needs) followed by retention of aggregate data only not identifiable to a natural person.

Based on applicable guidance and internal technical documentation, the controller considers that such processing — as actually configured — does not require prior consent through a cookie banner, without prejudice to the duty of transparency and the need to review compliance if technology or law changes.

3. Profiling and marketing cookies

As of the date of this update, the Site does not use profiling or marketing cookies to build profiles for personalised advertising or third-party retargeting. No social widgets are embedded that load third-party scripts for sharing or advertising tracking on the Site’s domain.

If such services are integrated in future, this policy will be updated and, where required by law, prior consent will be requested through appropriate means.

4. Third-party cookies and tools strictly related to service delivery or security

Some features may involve requests or data sent to third-party providers acting as independent controllers or as processors/sub-processors, as described in the Privacy Policy. Examples (non-exhaustive, depending on the published version of the Site) include:

  • anti-spam and bot protection services (e.g. CDN/WAF or human verification tools), where active;
  • embedded external content (e.g. interactive maps), where integrated;
  • delivery of fonts or libraries from third-party domains (e.g. Google Fonts), which may involve technical processing by the third party under its own policies.

In any event, in the configuration described at the date of this document, the controller does not use third-party advertising profiling cookies set on behalf of ad networks on the Site’s domain.

FAQ: technical cookies and banners

Question: must technical cookies be accepted via a banner to use the site?

Answer: strictly technical cookies (and tools functionally necessary to deliver the service requested by the user) generally do not require prior consent through a banner, but they still require a clear privacy notice. The Garante has clarified that information must be provided in a suitable way; a banner is a common tool but not the only one, provided the notice is actually accessible (e.g. visible link and dedicated text).

For these Sites, the absence of a banner is based on the absence, at the date of this update, of non-essential cookies requiring prior consent; this does not exempt the controller from transparency duties or from updating this text if the technical configuration changes.
