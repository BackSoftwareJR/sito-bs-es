8. Retention period

Personal data are stored for no longer than necessary to achieve the purposes for which they were collected, applying documented retention and deletion criteria. Indicatively:

  • Contractual purposes: data are kept until full performance of the contract and thereafter for periods required by civil, tax and accounting law (including mandatory document retention), unless another legal basis justifies further retention (e.g. ongoing disputes).

  • Consent-based processing: data are kept until withdrawal of consent or until the purpose for which consent was collected is achieved, unless another legal basis applies thereafter.
  • Legal obligations: the controller may retain data for longer periods if required by law, regulation or authority order.

At the end of the retention period, personal data are erased or irreversibly anonymised, unless the law requires retention in non-identifiable form or in aggregate archives.

Statistical processing, to the extent actually configured as described and not involving marketing profiling or third-party cross-site tracking, is documented internally and assessed against Garante guidance on cookies and tracking tools and the accountability principle.

12. Legal bases for processing

The controller processes personal data only where a legal basis under Arts. 6 and 9 GDPR exists, including by way of example:

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): where the user has freely given specific, informed and unambiguous consent for one or more specific purposes (e.g. newsletter subscription or processing explicitly requiring consent).

  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): where necessary for the purposes of legitimate interests pursued by the controller or a third party, except where such interests are overridden by the interests or fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject which require protection of personal data, in particular where the data subject is a child. Users may request clarification on the specific legal basis applied to each processing activity, within the limits of commercial confidentiality and security.

14. Data subject rights

Data subjects may exercise GDPR rights within the limits set by law, including:

  • Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent, without affecting lawfulness of processing based on consent before withdrawal.
  • Object to processing where processing is based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Access: obtain confirmation whether personal data concerning them are being processed and, if so, obtain information and a copy of the data, within legal limits.
  • Rectification and completion of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Restriction of processing in cases under Art. 18 GDPR.
  • Erasure («right to be forgotten») in cases under Art. 17 GDPR, subject to legal obligations or overriding grounds.
  • Data portability for data provided by the data subject, where Art. 20 GDPR applies.
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (in Italy, the Garante per la protezione dei dati personali: www.garanteprivacy.it) or seek judicial remedies.

Where personal data are processed for direct marketing purposes, the data subject has the right to object at any time to processing of personal data concerning them for such marketing, which includes profiling to the extent related to such direct marketing. Where the data subject objects, data will no longer be processed for those purposes, unless compelling legitimate grounds or legal defence apply.
